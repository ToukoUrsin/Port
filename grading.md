# Challenge

## The Challenge

Create innovative platforms, tools, or narratives that shape how people create, consume, and engage with culture and media in more responsible, inclusive, and impactful ways.

Consider representation, ethical storytelling, digital equity, sustainability of media systems, emerging technologies, and audience empowerment.

## Key Problems in This Space

1. **Algorithm echo chambers** - Recommendation systems optimize for engagement, not diversity. People get fed more of the same, leading to cultural homogenization and polarization.
2. **AI content flood** - Generative AI makes it trivial to produce content at scale, drowning out authentic human creators — especially underrepresented voices.
3. **Creator economy exploitation** - Platforms extract massive value from creators while paying them fractions. The power imbalance keeps growing.
4. **Cultural erasure** - Dominant platforms favor English-language, Western content. Local languages, traditions, and stories get pushed out.
5. **Misinformation & deepfakes** - Trust in media is eroding. Increasingly hard to distinguish authentic storytelling from manipulation.
6. **Accessibility gap** - Huge amounts of media remain inaccessible to people with disabilities (no captions, no audio descriptions, poor UX).
7. **Attention economy** - Media is designed to be addictive, not enriching. Engagement metrics reward outrage over substance.
8. **Digital divide in creation tools** - Professional media tools are expensive and complex, locking out voices from lower-income and rural communities.
9. **Media sustainability** - Streaming and data centers have enormous hidden carbon footprints.
10. **Audience passivity** - People consume more than ever but have almost no agency in shaping the narratives that affect their communities.

## GSSC 2025 Seoul — Competition Context

The PORT_ undergraduate track winner represents Aalto at the **Global Student Startup Competition (GSSC)** at the Asian Leadership Conference in Seoul.

- **2025 was the first GSSC** — 60 students from universities including Harvard, Stanford, UC Berkeley, Oxford, KAIST, and Aalto competed on the theme **"Human Centered AI"**
- **2025 winner:** Team REVITA (Seoul National University) — AI "smart scarecrow" for converting regular farms to smart farms
- **2026 theme:** "Next Generation of Culture & Media: Empowering Connection & Truth" — this aligns directly with the PORT_2026 challenge above

### What kinds of ideas tend to be rewarded

- **Real-world, human-centered problem solving** — ideas must address critical problems people actually face
- **Technical innovation with clear market application** — not just research, but a viable business
- **Social impact** — representation, digital equity, inclusion, sustainability
- **Scalability** — judges look for ideas that can grow beyond a niche
- **Strong storytelling** — pitch format rewards clarity and conviction

# Grading Criteria

| Criteria                         | Points |
|----------------------------------|--------|
| Technical Feasibility            | 10     |
| Market Potential and Scalability | 10     |
| Team Dynamics                    | 5      |
| Risk Evaluation                  | 5      |
| **Total**                        | **30** |

## Our Assumed Grading (based on GSSC 2025 winners & finalists)

| Criteria | What we think actually matters | Weight |
|----------|-------------------------------|--------|
| **Technical Feasibility (10p)** | Working prototype, not just slides. REVITA had hardware in a real potato field. EYE-TERRA had functional AI glasses. The 3-month pre-competition mentoring focuses on prototype development. Show you can build it. | Heaviest |
| **Market Potential & Scalability (10p)** | Real, large-scale problem with clear demand. REVITA targeted global agriculture crisis (aging farmers, labor shortage, climate change). Every finalist addressed problems affecting millions. Think globally, not just locally. | Heaviest |
| **Team Dynamics (5p)** | Interdisciplinary is key. REVITA had Plant Science + Industrial Engineering + AI. Small team (2 people) but complementary skills. Show you cover both tech and business. | Secondary |
| **Risk Evaluation (5p)** | Know your risks and have answers ready. The Q&A is where this shows. Acknowledge what could fail and how you'd handle it. | Secondary |

### Patterns from the winners

- **Social impact + technical depth wins** — judges explicitly praised EYE-TERRA for "combining technical innovation and social impact." Every finalist solved a human problem (farming, vision, autism).
- **Working tech > polished slides** — the winner had field-tested hardware, not a mockup.
- **2/3 of points are Technical + Market** — if your tech is real and the market is big, you're already at 20/30 before team and risk even matter.
- **The 2026 theme is culture & media** — mentors are from Disney+, Netflix, YouTube, TikTok, Duolingo, Techstars. Judges will think about entertainment/media industry viability.
- **Think about a real problem first** — every winning idea started from a painful, real problem (farmers can't manage fields, blind people can't navigate, autistic people need support), not from "cool tech looking for a use case."
