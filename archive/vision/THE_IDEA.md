## The Idea

Creating content is free. Checking if it's good costs $100. We made it cost 10 cents.

Six quality dimensions — perspectives, evidence, logic, framing, stakeholders, manipulation — checked at once. Before you publish. 3 seconds. One click.

It's a map, not a rulebook. Shows you the roads. You choose where to drive.

Spell-check killed typos by catching them while you write. We kill blind spots the same way.

## Why THIS Is THE Idea

Every cost-ratio crisis in history resolved the same way. Currency got watermarks. Food got HACCP. Email got spam filters. Credit cards got chips. Websites got HTTPS. 16 domains. 150+ years. Same pattern: when production becomes free and verification stays expensive, someone makes verification free too, embeds it in the creation process, and it becomes a standard.

Content is the last major domain where this hasn't happened. Because content quality was semantic — it required human understanding. No machine could do it. Until last year.

LLMs are the watermark moment for content. Imperfect but cheap automation replacing perfect but unscalable human judgment. This isn't a hypothesis. It's a pattern that has repeated across every resolved domain.

Nothing else works:
- "Fix the algorithms" — requires platform cooperation. They won't.
- "Educate consumers" — takes a generation. The crisis accelerates yearly.
- "More fact-checkers" — reactive. O(n) review vs exponential content. They lost.
- "Regulate platforms" — creates demand for quality signals without supply. We're the supply.
- "Make creation expensive" — kills the medium.

This version is THE version because:
- **Both sides want it.** Conservatives use it to prove CNN has gaps. Progressives use it to prove Fox has gaps. The disagreement IS the distribution.
- **Self-interest drives every adoption.** Nobody needs to care about society. Creator avoids embarrassment. Newsroom replaces laid-off editors. Platform avoids EU fines.
- **The selfish action IS the prosocial action.** No trade-off. Better articles AND a healthier ecosystem. Same action.
- **It claims an unclaimed layer.** Grammarly owns "does it read well?" SEMrush owns "will people find it?" Nobody owns "is it actually good?"

Why now: LLM capability just crossed the threshold. Institutional verification infrastructure is collapsing. The gap between demand and supply is the widest in history. This crossing happens once.

## The Virality

You score a Fox News article. 3/9 perspectives. You post the card. "Look what they're leaving out."

A conservative scores a CNN article. 4/9. Different gaps. They post it. "Look what YOUR side is leaving out."

Both posts get hundreds of shares. Every share has the URL. Every URL leads someone new to try it.

Then someone scores their OWN article. 52/100. They fix it. 74/100. They post both cards. "I didn't know I was missing this." That post gets more engagement than the attack posts because vulnerability is magnetic.

Three viral loops running simultaneously:

**The weapon loop** — "Look how blind THEY are." Political fuel. Endless.

**The mirror loop** — "Look what I discovered about MYSELF." Higher engagement.

**The competition loop** — "I scored 82. What did YOU get?" Wordle energy.

Each loop feeds the others. Weapon brings people in. Mirror converts them. Competition retains them.

The sharecard does all the work. One image: article title, score, biggest blind spot, URL. Every card is a complete ad. The users ARE the marketing team.

The question that spreads: **"Have you checked your score?"**

## What This Makes Possible

Once quality is visible on millions of articles, you're sitting on something nobody has ever had: a live map of what humanity knows, what it doesn't, and where the gaps are.

After a million checks on "AI regulation," you know that 94% of English-language articles miss the disability angle. That's not an opinion. That's a measurement. Nobody has ever been able to MEASURE blind spots at civilization scale.

**Quality-weighted search.** Google ranks by popularity. You rank by completeness. "Show me the most thorough article on housing affordability" — not the most clicked, the most COMPLETE.

**The perspective weather report.** "Coverage of the earthquake is at 31/100 right now — missing infrastructure, disability access, economic impact. By hour 6: 58." Real-time quality monitoring during breaking news.

**Institutional quality benchmarking.** Not bias ratings — COMPLETENESS ratings. "This outlet averages 72/100. That one averages 48." More defensible than bias scoring because you're measuring what's present, not what's "correct."

**Personal quality tracking.** "Over the last year, your articles averaged 67/100. Weakest dimension: stakeholder coverage. Strongest: evidence." A fitness tracker for intellectual thoroughness.

**Cross-cultural perspective mapping.** Score the same topic across 30 countries. See not just what different cultures write about — but what each culture CANNOT SEE. The systematic blind spots of entire civilizations, made visible for the first time.

**The AI content quality loop.** AI generates an article. The tool scores it. 44/100. AI regenerates with gaps filled. 78/100. Quality check becomes part of the AI writing pipeline. Content quality improves automatically, without human intervention.

**The quality marketplace.** Advertisers pay 3x more to appear next to content scoring above 80. Quality becomes economically rewarded — not because anyone decided it should be, but because the measurement exists and money follows measurement.
