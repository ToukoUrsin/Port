# BUSINESS MODEL: Creator Wedge to Platform Infrastructure

## The Creator Wedge

Creators are the ONLY users who span the full market stack simultaneously:

| Layer | Consumer | Creator | Editor | Compliance |
|-------|----------|---------|--------|------------|
| Product surface | Visits for curiosity | PRODUCES output here | Uses as tool | Uses as report |
| Quality mechanisms | Doesn't go here | MUST understand to create quality | MUST understand to edit | Reports on |
| Symptoms (overwhelm, distrust) | Feels pain | Feels pain AND reputation depends on quality | Responsible for fixing | Mandated to fix |
| Infrastructure | Doesn't know it exists | Competes in the attention market | Reports to owners | Regulated by law |

The creator is the bridge between vitamin (consumer) and painkiller (professional):
- They have consumer-level accessibility (one person, no procurement)
- But professional-level pain (their reputation/income depends on not missing things)
- They access the product at their WORKFLOW moment (before publishing = recurring trigger)
- Proven willingness to pay (Grammarly: $1.5B ARR on exactly this pattern)

### Why Creator-First Beats Education-First

## Retention Loops

### Loop 1: Pre-Publish Check (Creator -- the Grammarly loop)

```
EVERY PUBLISH CYCLE:
  Creator writes draft -> Runs check -> Sees blind spots -> Improves draft -> Publishes
  (next week, repeat)
```

Frequency: Weekly for newsletter writers. Daily for newsrooms. Monthly for long-form. The trigger is external (their own publish schedule), not internal (our notification). This is the strongest retention pattern -- we don't have to remind them. Their workflow does.

### Loop 2: Topic Change Alerts (Consumer + Creator)

```
Day 1:  User analyzes "EU AI Act implications" -> verification level: 72 (hot, contested)
Week 2: Alert: "Verification dropped to 55.
        2 claims debunked. 1 new perspective appeared (labor unions)."
Week 6: Alert: "Verification at 38. Approaching consensus on 3 of 5 claims.
        Gap closed: disability access perspective now represented."
```

The come-back trigger: the topic changed shape, and you want to see the new map. This is the Twitter notification loop ("someone replied") applied to knowledge evolution ("reality shifted").

### Loop 3: Coverage Dashboard (Content Team)

```
WEEKLY:
  Content lead opens dashboard -> Sees team's coverage gaps -> Assigns stories -> Repeat
```

Retention through workflow integration. Once embedded in the editorial meeting, switching costs are high.

## Nested Moats (4 Layers of Defensibility)

The moat is not the LLM call -- anyone can replicate a single API call. The moat is four nested layers, each starting from day one and deepening over time:

```
TIME          LAYER    MOAT DESCRIPTION                          STRENGTH
--------      -----    ----------------------------------        -----------
Month 1-6     Layer 1  UX patterns: what visualizations          Weak
                       work, how users navigate, what
                       topics they analyze

Month 6-12    Layer 2  Calibrated perspective taxonomies:        Moderate
                       "for technology topics, the 7
                       standard perspectives are..."
                       Built from 50K+ analyses.

Month 12-18   Layer 3  Systematic gap patterns: "the             Strong
                       disability perspective is missing
                       from 73% of tech coverage." Meta-
                       analysis across topic categories.

Month 18-36   Layer 4  Evolution data: how topics develop        Very strong
                       over time, which perspectives
                       emerge at what stage, cross-topic
                       structural patterns.
```

The moat isn't "delayed 2 years." The moat starts building from analysis #1 and deepens through the layers over time. A competitor entering at month 12 is missing 6 months of Layer 1 data AND 6 months of Layer 2 calibration. By month 18, the gap is three layers deep.

**Pitch line:** "Every analysis builds our perspective database. After a month, we know what visualizations work. After six months, we have calibrated taxonomies for thousands of topics. After a year, we know the systematic blind spots in media coverage. That data is unreplicable."

Additionally, the moat includes:
- Domain-specific quality receipts
- Longitudinal quality histories per publisher
- Organization-specific policy layers
- Workflow distribution (CMS plugin integrations = switching costs)

## The Adoption Cascade

Nobody needs to be altruistic. Nobody needs to "care about the media crisis." Each actor adopts because the PREVIOUS phase created a self-interested reason to do so.

```
Phase 1 (Month 1-6):   Creators adopt to avoid embarrassment + competitive edge
Phase 2 (Month 6-12):  Creators show scores -> readers expect quality signals ->
                        more creators adopt to avoid looking unchecked
Phase 3 (Month 12-24): Newsrooms notice reporters already using it individually ->
                        buy team plan -> quality becomes measurable
Phase 4 (Month 18-36): Platforms face regulatory pressure (DSA) + creator demand +
                        advertiser demand -> integrate API
Phase 5 (Month 36-60): Multiple platforms use same scoring -> de facto standard ->
                        regulatory bodies reference it
```

Each phase creates the incentive for the next. The label creates the standard. The standard creates the lock-in.

**Pitch:** "We're not building a tool. We're building the standard for content quality. Like FICO for credit or Nutrition Facts for food. The EU DSA is the regulatory tailwind. We'll be ready."
