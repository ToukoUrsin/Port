## THE JUDGE'S EMOTIONAL JOURNEY

**Hour 1 (pitches 1-5):** Alert, generous, comparing each to "good hackathon project."

**Hour 2 (pitches 6-10):** Pattern recognition. "Another AI chatbot." "Another content tool." The bar for standing out rises.

**Hour 3 (pitches 11+):** Fatigue. Only the remarkable survives. Visual memory > verbal memory. They're looking for REASONS TO REMEMBER, not reasons to score well.

**Deliberation:** One judge must ADVOCATE. "I really liked the team that ___." If they can't fill in the blank, the team loses.

**Implication:** Our pitch must survive hour-3 fatigue. Visual > verbal. The live demo + judges opening the URL on their phones = multi-sensory, non-forgettable.

## HIGH-LEVEL AGENDA: THE 4-MINUTE PITCH

### Structure: 7 Slides, 4 Minutes

We have 4 minutes (not 3 — confirmed). ~600 words of speaking. 7 slides. The demo lives INSIDE the presentation, not as a separate detour.

```
SLIDE 1: THE HOOK                    [0:00 - 0:25]   25 sec
SLIDE 2: THE INSIGHT                 [0:25 - 0:50]   25 sec
SLIDE 3: THE PRODUCT (overview)      [0:50 - 1:10]   20 sec
SLIDE 4: THE LIVE DEMO               [1:10 - 2:20]   70 sec  ← THE PEAK
SLIDE 5: THE QUALITY ENGINE          [2:20 - 2:50]   30 sec  ← EMOTIONAL PEAK
SLIDE 6: THE ECONOMICS + MARKET      [2:50 - 3:20]   30 sec
SLIDE 7: THE CLOSE                   [3:20 - 4:00]   40 sec
```

Total: 240 seconds. Demo gets 100 seconds (slides 4+5). That's 42% of the pitch.

### Slide-by-Slide Breakdown

---

#### SLIDE 1: THE HOOK [0:00 - 0:25]

**Visual:** Full-screen number: **213** fading to a dark background. Then: a map showing news deserts in grey.

**Script:** "213 counties in America have zero local journalists. Not one. When the newspaper closes, corruption rises 7%. Not because corrupt people move in — because nobody's watching anymore. 50 million people live in communities where nobody writes down what happens."

**What it does:** Creates emotional gravity. The problem is real, measured, and specific. Not abstract — "corruption rises 7%" is a peer-reviewed number that lands.

**Challenge thread:** This is why the challenge asks for **sustainability of media systems** — because the current system has collapsed. And why **representation** matters — whole communities are invisible when nobody covers them.

**Design:** Dark background. Big numbers. Minimal text. The speaker carries the weight, not the slide.

---

#### SLIDE 2: THE INSIGHT [0:25 - 0:50]

**Visual:** Split screen. Left: a Facebook post about a council meeting (messy, informal, 3 likes, disappears). Right: same information as a proper news article. Arrow from left to right: "AI."

**Script:** "But the information isn't gone. People know what happened at the town hall. They see the new business open. They share it on Facebook groups that disappear in 24 hours. The knowledge is there — scattered, unstructured, and ephemeral. Nobody turns it into stories people actually read. Until now."

**What it does:** Reframes the problem from "lack of information" to "lack of structure." This is the insight moment — the thing the audience hadn't articulated.

**Challenge thread:** This is the **audience empowerment** gap. People already CREATE culture/media content (Facebook posts, voice messages). But they can't CREATE it responsibly or impactfully — because there's no structure, no quality layer, no permanence. The challenge asks us to shape how people create. We do: by turning their raw voice into journalism.

**Design:** Clean split. Left side messy/social-media-looking. Right side clean/newspaper-looking. The contrast IS the message.

#### SLIDE 3: THE PRODUCT [0:50 - 1:10]

**Visual:** Three simple icons in a row: Microphone → AI brain → Newspaper. Below: "Record. Generate. Publish. 5 minutes."

**Script:** "We built [Name]. Record a voice memo. Snap some photos. AI writes a real news article — structured, with attributed quotes, proper context. A second AI pass reviews it: are the facts grounded? Whose voice is missing? You hit publish. Your town has a newspaper again."

**What it does:** The product in 20 seconds. Three verbs. Simple visual. The detail comes in the demo.

**Challenge thread:** This slide embodies **three challenge verbs at once**. CREATE: anyone creates journalism with their phone. CONSUME: the town newspaper is the consumption layer. ENGAGE: the contributor IS the community, not a distant professional. Plus **digital equity** (no expensive equipment, no training) and **emerging technologies** (the pipeline is only possible now with LLMs + STT).

**Design:** Minimal. The three icons should feel like a natural pipeline. No feature lists. No screenshots yet.

#### SLIDE 4: THE LIVE DEMO [1:10 - 2:20]

**Visual:** Switch from slides to the LIVE APP. Samu's pre-recorded video plays for 20-30 seconds (sped up: him interviewing a team, taking photos, recording). Then the app is shown.

**Script flow:**
1. [0:00-0:25] "Let me show you. Yesterday, Samu reported this hackathon." → Play 20-25 sec sped-up video of Samu walking PORT_, interviewing teams, snapping photos.
2. [0:25-0:45] "He spent 10 minutes. Here's what happened." → Show the app with raw inputs. Hit Generate. SSE events tick through on screen: "Listening to your recording..." "Writing your article..." "Reviewing..."
3. [0:45-1:00] Article appears. Scroll through it briefly. Judges see a real, well-structured article about the event they're sitting in. "Five seconds from voice memo to published article."
4. [1:00-1:10] "But here's the part that matters..." → transition to quality engine (slide 5)

**What it does:** This is the TRANSFORMATION MOMENT. The Kronos parallel. Raw voice memo → real news article, in real time, about the room they're in. Every detail is verifiable. The demo IS the product.

**Challenge thread:** This is **emerging technologies** made tangible — not as a slide claiming "we use AI" (every team does), but as a live demonstration that LLMs + STT make a new kind of media creation possible. And it's the strongest possible proof of **audience empowerment** — a student with a phone just created real journalism.

**Design decisions:**
- Pre-cache the generation if API latency is risky. The SSE animation can play from cached data.
- The article must look like a NEWSPAPER, not a web app. Typography matters enormously.
- The sped-up video must be polished (30 seconds max, energetic cuts).

**Fallback:** If the live generation fails, show a pre-generated article and say "we generated this earlier from Samu's recording." Less powerful but still works.

## THE DECK'S DESIGN LANGUAGE

### Visual Principles

1. **Dark backgrounds, light text.** Better contrast on projectors. More dramatic. Feels premium.
2. **One idea per slide.** Never compete with the speaker.
3. **Big numbers.** 213. $300K. $5. 64→81. Numbers are visual when large enough.
4. **No bullet lists.** Each point is its own visual moment.
5. **Newspaper typography for article display.** Serif font. Clean columns. The article should LOOK like journalism.
6. **Minimal branding.** Product name in corner. No logos competing with content.

### Font Guidance
- Slide text: clean sans-serif (Inter, Helvetica, SF Pro)
- Article display: serif (Georgia, Playfair Display) — must look like a newspaper
- Numbers: big, bold, contrasting color

### Color Palette
- Background: near-black (#0a0a0a) or deep navy (#0f172a)
- Text: white (#ffffff) or warm white (#f8fafc)
- Accent: warm amber/gold for key numbers and highlights
- Quality scores: green/yellow/red traffic light system
- The article view: white background with dark text (newspaper feel — contrasts with dark slides)

| "How is this different from Patch?" | "Patch aggregates existing sources. In 28,000 of their 30,000 communities, there's no original reporting — just AI summaries of other people's journalism. We create original content from citizen contributions." |
