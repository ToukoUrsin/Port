# SPEAKER NOTES — 4-Minute Pitch
# Date: 2026-03-08 UTC+3

For the v3 deck (uutiset.pdf, 12 slides). These are practical notes — what to say and when.

---

## RECOMMENDED SLIDE ORDER

The deck PDF has slides 1-12. Reorder the middle section for better flow:

```
Slides 1-7    As-is (problem → solution → how it works)
— LIVE DEMO —
Slide 10      Quality engine (right after demo — it's the payoff)
Slide 9       Traction
Slide 8       GTM
Slide 11      Economics
Slide 12      Close
```

Quality must come right after the demo because it explains what the judges just saw. Traction/GTM/economics are the business case — group them together.

## SLIDE 6 — Phone with REC [0:38 - 0:48]

## LIVE DEMO [1:00 - 1:50]

### Samu's input (~15s)

If video: play it while explaining:

> "Samu walked this floor for ten minutes. Talked to a team. Took photos. Recorded what he saw."

If no video, show the raw inputs in the app:

> "A two-minute voice memo. Three photos. A short note."

### Generation (~15s)

> "That's the whole input. Watch."

Hit Generate. Don't talk during the SSE events — let "Listening..." "Writing..." "Reviewing..." build anticipation. Article appears.

> "Eighteen seconds."

Scroll through. Point at a quote:

> "This came straight from the transcript. The AI didn't invent it."

### Quality discovery (~20s)

> "See this? Sixty-four out of a hundred. The AI didn't just write the article — it reviewed it. Two of five stakeholder voices represented. Missing: the judges' perspective."

> "Samu grabbed a judge. Thirty seconds of audio. Regenerated."

Show the update: 81/100.

> "Thirty seconds to add the missing voice. The AI asked a question most editors never ask: whose voice is missing from this story?"

That's it. Don't over-explain. The contrast with "hackathon project" expectations does the work.

## SLIDE 11 — Economics [2:30 - 2:42]

Let the visual contrast land.

## SLIDE 12 — Close [2:42 - 3:15]

Slow down here. This is conversational, not a crescendo.

> "Every community has stories worth telling. The grandmother's wisdom. The school play. The council fight. They happened and vanished."

> "AI doesn't just fix the economics. It gives every community a voice."

> "Your town gets a voice. The world gets to hear it."

> "Check it. It's live right now. news dot minir dot ai."

Stop. URL on screen. Let judges pull out their phones. The remaining time is them reading Samu's article on their own devices.

## PRACTICAL NOTES

- Don't open with "Hi everyone" or introductions. First word is "Two."
- Slides 1-3 are fast. Slide 4 is where you first slow down.
- During demo generation, silence is fine. Don't fill it.
- The bakery/accusation line is the single best summary of the quality engine. Land it clearly.
- "Check it. It's live right now." — say this to the judges, not to the screen.
- If you blank, read what's on the slide and riff from there. The slides carry the key lines.

## IF THINGS GO WRONG

| Problem | Fix |
|---|---|
| API down | Show pre-generated article: "We generated this from Samu's recording earlier." |
| Quality flag is boring | Switch to a pre-reviewed article with a better flag. |
| Website down | Screenshots. "The site is having a moment — here's what it looks like." |
| WiFi dies | Mobile hotspot. If that dies, screenshots. |
| Running over | Cut GTM to one sentence. Don't cut demo or close. |
| Running under | Stretch the pauses. Scroll the article slower. |

## THE RETELLING TEST

After all pitches, one judge says to another:

> "Did you see the team that reported the hackathon live? Their AI caught missing voices. It's live on my phone."

Three memories: (1) reported the hackathon, (2) AI caught missing voices, (3) it's live on my phone. Everything you say serves these three.
