# Who Is This For? Who Pays?

---

## The Users

### Contributors (anyone in the community)

The person who was at the town hall meeting. The parent who saw what happened at the school board. The neighbor who noticed the new business opening. The grandmother with a story about the town's history.

They have raw material — a recording, photos, notes. They want it to matter. Today they'd post on Facebook and it disappears. We give it structure, quality, and permanence.

**Why they'd contribute for free:** Same reason people post on local Facebook groups, Nextdoor, and community WhatsApp. People already share local information for free. We're channeling energy that exists, not creating it. The difference: their contribution becomes a real article with their name on it, not a post that vanishes.

### Readers (everyone in the community)

Anyone who wants to know what's happening locally. They open the town's newspaper, scan headlines, read what matters. No account needed.

Today their options are: scattered Facebook posts, Nextdoor gossip, or nothing. We give them a clean, structured source for local information.

---

## Who Pays

### Local businesses (primary, small amounts that add up)

A pizza shop paying $5/month to be featured. A hardware store sponsoring the "Business" section. Even tiny amounts work because our cost per town is under $5/year. Most towns have 10-50 small businesses.

### Municipalities (secondary, higher value)

Towns pay $50-200/month for enhanced community communication — official announcements, meeting summaries, public notices published automatically. This replaces the $2,000/year they spend on physical notice boards and mailing.

### Premium contributors (tertiary)

Contributors who want analytics, scheduling, or multi-town publishing pay $5-10/month.

### Aggregate advertising (at scale)

Once we have 1,000+ towns, we sell regional/national ad inventory programmatically. This is where real revenue starts.

**Scaling math:**
- 1,000 towns x $100/month average = $1.2M ARR
- 10,000 towns x $150/month = $18M ARR
- Costs scale sub-linearly (AI costs drop yearly, infrastructure shared)

## Competition

| What exists | What it does | Why we're different |
|---|---|---|
| Facebook Groups | Unstructured community posts | We add structure, quality review, journalistic standards |
| Nextdoor | Neighborhood social network | We produce articles, not social media posts |
| Patch | Professional hyperlocal news | They need professional journalists ($$$). We use AI + community |
| Lokal (YC S19) | Local news platform, India | We add AI-powered creation and editorial review |
| ChatGPT/Claude directly | AI can write anything | No publishing platform, no community, no quality review, no distribution |

**Our position:** We turn community chatter into community journalism. A Facebook group post about a council meeting is noise. An AI-structured article with quality review is signal.

---

## Validation

**Lokal (YC S19)** built a local news platform for India's small towns. Raised funding, proved local news works as a platform business in underserved markets. They succeeded by making content creation cheap through user-generated content with lightweight editorial. We go further — AI handles the editorial layer entirely.

## Where It Goes

**Phase 1:** English-speaking news deserts (US rural, UK small towns, Australia). Prove the model.

**Phase 2:** Any language. Pipeline works in 99 languages via Whisper + Claude. Finland, India, South America.

**Phase 3:** Beyond geography. Campus newspapers, company internal news, religious communities. Any group where things happen but nobody writes them up.

**Phase 4:** The data layer. Thousands of towns producing stories daily = the most granular community-level dataset worldwide. Local signals surfacing state/national patterns.
