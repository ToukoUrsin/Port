# Market Size: The TAM That Didn't Exist Until Now

*Research date: 2026-03-07 UTC+3*

The local news market was never small. It was expensive. At $300K/year per town, only large communities could afford newspapers. At $5/year per town, every community on earth is addressable.

This document proves market size not through projections but through countable things.

---

## 1. Count the Communities

### United States
- **3,142 counties** total
- **213 counties** are complete news deserts — zero local journalists
- **~1,700 counties** have lost at least one newspaper since 2005
- **Half of all counties** have one local news outlet or fewer
- **55 million Americans** have limited or no access to local news
- **3,200+ newspapers** have closed or merged since 2004

### Brazil
- **5,570 municipalities** total
- **2,968 (53.3%)** have no media at all
- **29.3 million people** affected

### Portugal
- **308 municipalities** total
- **25%** have no local media outlet
- **53.9%** are at risk of becoming news deserts

### European Union
- All **27 member states** affected — no country immune
- First EU-wide study (CMPF, 2024) assessed risks across 55 variables
- Central/Eastern Europe and rural areas worst hit
- **UK**: 320+ local titles closed (2009-2019), 6,000+ fewer journalists

### Latin America
- News deserts exceed **65% of territory** studied across Argentina, Chile, Colombia, Mexico, Peru

### Finland (our launch market)
- **308 municipalities**
- **18 municipalities** have zero media coverage
- **180+ local papers** still exist but consolidating rapidly — down from 320 titles (2013) to 274 (2022)
- Rural journalists being moved to regional centers; peripheral coverage thinning
- Monopoly consolidation: most regions now have a single newspaper group owning all local titles

### Global
- **~4 million** towns and cities exist in comprehensive databases
- No universal count of news deserts, but the pattern is universal: professional local journalism is economically unviable in small communities

**Conservative estimate of addressable communities worldwide: 500,000+**
(Every municipality under 50K population in countries where local news infrastructure has declined)

## 2. Revenue Per Community

Three revenue layers, each independently viable at our cost structure:

### Layer 1: Local Business Advertising
- A town of 2,000 has 10-50 small businesses
- A featured listing costs €5-20/month per business
- Conservative: 5 advertisers × €10/month = **€50/month per town**
- US local digital advertising exceeded **$100 billion in 2024** (70% of all local ad spend)
- 52% of small businesses have monthly marketing budgets under $1,000 — we fit in the smallest budget

### Layer 2: Municipal Subscriptions
- Towns pay €50-200/month for enhanced communication: official announcements, meeting summaries, public notices published automatically
- Replaces physical notice boards, mailings, dead PDF newsletters
- 78% of residents support tax dollars spent on communication tools (CivicPlus research)
- Conservative: **€100/month per subscribing municipality**

### Layer 3: Aggregate Advertising (at scale)
- At 1,000+ towns, sell regional/national ad inventory programmatically
- At 10,000+ towns, the network becomes a unique local advertising channel
- Global ad spend surpassed **$1 trillion in 2025**, growing 7%/year
- Location-based advertising alone: **$56.9 billion** in 2025

## 3. The TAM Calculation

### Bottom-Up (Conservative)

| Scope | Communities | Avg Revenue/Month | Annual Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Finland** (launch) | 308 | €75 | €277K |
| **Nordics** | ~1,200 | €75 | €1.1M |
| **US news deserts** | ~1,700 | $100 | $2.0M |
| **US all small towns** | ~15,000 | $100 | $18M |
| **EU underserved** | ~50,000 | €75 | €45M |
| **Global** (500K communities) | 500,000 | $50 | **$300M** |

These are conservative — they assume only ad revenue and municipal subscriptions, no premium features, no data licensing, no aggregate ad network effects.

### Top-Down

Local news generated **$23 billion** in US advertising revenue at peak (2006). That revenue didn't disappear — it redistributed to Facebook, Google, and Nextdoor. We recapture a fraction of it at 1/10,000th the cost structure.

Even capturing **1%** of the redistributed US local news ad revenue = **$230M**.

### The Cost-Creates-Market Argument

Traditional local news TAM was limited by cost structure:
- Only towns that could support $200K+/year in revenue were addressable
- That's roughly towns over 20,000 population
- ~5,000 communities in the US

Our TAM is limited by existence:
- Any community with things happening and people living there
- That's every town, village, and neighborhood on earth
- Cost per community: under $5/year in AI costs

**The market didn't shrink. The cost structure excluded 95% of it. We include it.**

## 4. The Hidden Economic Value

News deserts don't just lack news — they cost money:

| Effect | Source | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Municipal borrowing costs rise | Gao, Lee & Murphy (2020) | **+5-11 basis points = $650K extra per bond issue** |
| Multiple bonds compound | Same study | Can reach **$1.95M per municipality** at 30bp |
| Corporate violations increase | Heese, Cavazos & Peter (HBS) | Financial penalties up **15%** |
| Toxic emissions increase | Jiang & Kong (2023) | **+10%** — online media doesn't mitigate this |
| Government transparency drops | Posner-Ferdman & Cuillier (2025) | States with fewer newspapers have worse public records compliance |

**The ROI calculation:**
- Average US municipality in a news desert: $650K+ in excess borrowing costs per bond issue
- Our cost to serve that municipality: ~$5/year
- **ROI: 130,000:1** on a single bond issue

For Finnish municipalities: even a modest borrowing cost reduction pays for the entire platform across all 308 municipalities for centuries.

This isn't a media product. It's democratic infrastructure with a measurable financial return.
