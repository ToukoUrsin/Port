## THE 7 CANDIDATE AUDIENCES (Concrete People, Not Abstractions)

### A. The Substack Writer (Individual Creator)

**Who:** Someone with 500-5,000 subscribers writing about a topic they care about (AI policy, climate, health, education). They're not a journalist. They're a domain expert who writes.

**Tuesday afternoon problem:** "I just finished a 2,000-word draft about AI hiring bias. Before I hit publish, I want to know: am I missing something obvious? I don't want to get destroyed in the comments by someone pointing out a perspective I should have covered."

**Trigger:** The moment between "draft done" and "hit publish"
**Willingness to pay:** YES. Creators already pay $12-30/mo for writing tools (Grammarly, Hemingway, Jasper). $15/mo is within range.
**Workflow insertion:** Post-draft, pre-publish. One click. 30 seconds of review.

### B. The Newsroom Editor (Small Group)

**Who:** An editor at a mid-size outlet (50-500K readers) managing 3-10 writers. Responsible for coverage balance.

**Tuesday afternoon problem:** "We published 12 articles this week about the housing crisis. Are we covering all the angles, or are we stuck in our ideological lane? I need to show the editorial board we're balanced."

**Trigger:** Weekly editorial meeting. Monthly coverage review.
**Willingness to pay:** YES. $500/mo is a rounding error on a newsroom budget. This is compliance/quality tooling.
**Workflow insertion:** Dashboard view. Weekly report auto-generated.

### C. The University Student (Individual Consumer)

**Who:** A 20-year-old writing a term paper on immigration policy. Needs to show "multiple perspectives" for the assignment.

**Tuesday afternoon problem:** "My professor said I need to cover at least 4 perspectives. I can think of 2. What am I missing?"

**Trigger:** Assignment deadline. Research phase.
**Willingness to pay:** WEAK. Students don't pay for tools. Freemium or institutional license.
**Workflow insertion:** Research phase. Before writing.

### D. The Policy Researcher (Small Group / Community)

**Who:** Someone at a think tank or government body preparing a policy brief. Must demonstrate comprehensive stakeholder analysis.

**Tuesday afternoon problem:** "I'm writing a brief on AI regulation for the ministry. I need to show we've considered all affected stakeholders. If I miss one, the opposition will use it against us."

**Trigger:** Policy brief deadline. Stakeholder mapping phase.
**Willingness to pay:** YES. Government/think tank budgets. $500-2,000/mo for team tools.
**Workflow insertion:** Early research phase. Stakeholder identification.

### E. The PR/Comms Professional (Individual / Small Group)

**Who:** Someone preparing a company's response to a public issue (layoffs, controversy, product recall). Needs to anticipate how different audiences will react.

**Tuesday afternoon problem:** "We're announcing 500 layoffs next week. I need to draft a statement that doesn't get destroyed on Twitter. What perspectives will people react from?"

**Trigger:** Crisis communication. Product launch. Public statement.
**Willingness to pay:** HIGH. PR budgets are large. Crisis = urgent. $500-5,000/engagement.
**Workflow insertion:** Pre-statement review. "Run it through the perspective check."

### F. The Podcast Host / Interviewer (Individual Creator)

**Who:** Someone preparing for an interview on a complex topic. Needs to understand all angles so they can ask better questions.

**Tuesday afternoon problem:** "I'm interviewing an AI ethicist tomorrow. I want to ask questions from perspectives the ethicist won't bring up voluntarily. What angles am I missing?"

**Trigger:** Interview prep. Day before recording.
**Willingness to pay:** MODERATE. Serious podcasters pay for research tools. $15/mo fits.
**Workflow insertion:** Prep phase. 1-2 hours before the interview.

### G. The DEI/Inclusion Lead (Small Group / Community)

**Who:** Someone responsible for diversity, equity, and inclusion at a company or institution. Must demonstrate comprehensive perspective coverage.

**Tuesday afternoon problem:** "We're designing a new employee policy. My job is to make sure we've considered all affected groups. If we miss a perspective, it's my neck on the line."

**Trigger:** Policy design. Program review. Annual audit.
**Willingness to pay:** YES. DEI budgets exist. $500/mo for tooling is standard.
**Workflow insertion:** Policy design phase. Stakeholder review.

## THE SWEET SPOT: THE SUBSTACK WRITER

### Why This Audience Wins

**1. Highest frequency + highest pain + perfect workflow fit.**

They publish weekly. Every week is a new opportunity to be publicly wrong. The fear of "getting ratio'd for missing an obvious perspective" is REAL and RECURRING. The tool inserts at the exact moment of maximum anxiety: draft done, finger hovering over "publish."

**2. The workflow insertion is the simplest possible.**

They're ALREADY in a text editor. They copy their draft. They paste it. They get back: "5 of 8 perspectives covered. Missing: [list]." They decide whether to add anything. They publish.

No new workflow. No dashboard. No setup. No onboarding. Copy. Paste. Read. Decide.

**3. The growth path is organic.**

Substack writers TALK TO EACH OTHER. They share tools. "I use this thing that checks my blind spots before I publish" spreads through the creator community. Word of mouth, not paid acquisition.

**4. The demo is trivially showable.**

Take a real Substack post. Paste it. Show the gaps. The judge READS THE GAPS and thinks "oh, they really are missing that." The demo proves itself.

**5. The pricing is proven.**

Creators pay $12/mo for Grammarly (grammar). $20/mo for Jasper (AI writing). $15/mo for blind spot checking is in the exact right range.

**6. The expansion path is clear.**

Substack writers → Medium writers → newsletter writers → all content creators → newsrooms → platforms. Each expansion moves one ring outward in the market.

### The Specific Product for This Audience

**Core flow:**
1. Writer finishes draft
2. Copies text (or connects Substack/Google Docs)
3. Pastes into the tool
4. Gets back in 3-5 seconds:
   - **Topic identified:** "AI hiring bias"
   - **Perspectives covered:** Tech industry (strong), Legal/regulatory (moderate), Affected workers (mentioned)
   - **Perspectives missing:** Disability community, Small business, HR practitioners, Academic researchers, Global South labor
   - **Biggest blind spot:** "You don't address how AI hiring tools interact with disability disclosure — 26% of US adults have a disability, and automated screening disproportionately filters them out."
   - **Evidence state:** 3 of 5 covered perspectives have strong sourcing. 2 rely on single sources.
5. Writer decides: add the disability angle (30 min more work) or publish as-is knowing the gap
6. Publishes with more confidence either way

**What it is NOT:**
- Not a writing assistant (doesn't write for you)
- Not a fact-checker (doesn't verify claims)
- Not an editor (doesn't fix prose)
- Not a political bias detector (doesn't do left/right)

**It is ONE thing:** It tells you what perspectives you're missing on the topic you wrote about.

### The Smallest Useful Unit

The SMALLEST version that provides value:

Input: text
Output: a list of covered perspectives + a list of missing perspectives + one specific blind spot sentence

That's it. No map. No radar. No animation. No mirror. Just the list.

Build time for just the list: **4-6 hours** (LLM prompt + basic React form + styled output).

Everything else (map, radar, mirror, animation, evidence labels) is POLISH on top of this core.

## WHAT ABOUT THE OTHER SOLUTION PRIMITIVES?

How do 10 proposed product primitives map to the Substack Writer audience?

| Primitive | Relevant? | How? |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| 1. Prism Mirror (map + your position) | OPTIONAL — polish, not core | Nice visual but the list is the value |
| 2. Context Receipt (shareable card) | YES — "share your coverage score" | Growth mechanic: writers share their scores |
| 3. Blindspot Radar (compare two sources) | NO — writers don't compare outlets | Different audience (readers) |
| 4. Anti-Feed Reader | NO — writers aren't scrolling | Different problem |
| 5. Claim Trail | NO — writers aren't fact-checking | Different tool |
| 6. Temporal Audit | NO — not relevant to pre-publish | Different problem |
| 7. Perspective Audit for Teams | FUTURE — when we expand to newsrooms | Expansion path, not MVP |
| 8. Epistemic Weather | NO — too abstract | Different audience |
| 9. Social Response Map | ADJACENT — "how will people react?" | Could be a feature, not core |
| 10. Motivation Lens | NO — not relevant to pre-publish | Different problem |

**Only 2 of 10 primitives matter for the Substack Writer:** the core perspective analysis (#1 stripped to its essence) and the shareable Context Receipt (#2, as a growth mechanic).

This is the power of going specific. 8 of 10 interesting ideas become irrelevant when you commit to a specific audience. The remaining 2 become extremely focused.
