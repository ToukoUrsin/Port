# The Mission — What the Article Engine Is Really For
# Date: 2026-03-07 UTC+3

---

## The Goal

The contributor who wrote the article feels proud.
The people who read it feel interested.
The community it's about feels represented.

That's it. Everything in the engine serves these three feelings.

---

## The AI as Mentor, Not Machine

The AI is not a content generator. It's the best editor you've ever worked with — someone who genuinely wants YOUR article to be great. Not their article. Yours.

A great editor doesn't rewrite your piece. They ask the right questions:

- "You mentioned the new playground — did the kids have a reaction? That could be the heart of the story."
- "The council voted 5-2 but you didn't mention who dissented. Do you know? That detail makes this twice as interesting."
- "You talked about the shop owner for three paragraphs but never quoted her. Can you go back and ask her one question? Even 30 seconds would transform this."
- "This is really good. The part about the grandmother's recipe is the strongest section. Can you expand it?"

The AI sees what the contributor can't see about their own work — not because the contributor is bad, but because everyone has blind spots. The AI's job is to make the contributor's voice shine, not to replace it.

---

## The Back-and-Forth

The pipeline is not: input → output → done.

It's a conversation:

```
1. Contributor records voice memo, takes photos, adds notes
2. AI generates a first draft from their input
3. AI reviews the draft and identifies what would make it GREAT:
   - "You have 3 quotes but they're all from the same person.
     A second voice would make this feel complete."
   - "The opening is burying your best detail. The part about
     the 90-year-old showing up — lead with that."
   - "Your photos show the crowd but the article doesn't mention
     how many people came. Do you remember roughly?"
4. Contributor sees the suggestions. They can:
   - Add a 30-second voice clip answering the AI's question
   - Type a quick note ("about 50 people, mostly families")
   - Ignore the suggestion (their choice, always)
5. AI regenerates with the new input. Score improves.
6. Maybe one more round. Maybe they're happy already.
7. Publish.
```

The key: the AI asks for what's MISSING, not what's WRONG. It's additive, not critical. "This would be even better with..." not "This is missing..."

## What Each Person Should Feel

### The Contributor

**Before:** "I saw something interesting but I'm not a writer. I wouldn't know how to turn this into an article."

**During:** "The AI took my rambling voice memo and made it sound professional. And it asked me one question I hadn't thought of — who else was affected? I went back and got a 30-second quote from the neighbor. Now the article has two voices instead of one."

**After:** "I wrote that. My name is on it. My neighbors are reading it. The AI helped, but the story is mine — I was there, I recorded it, I chose what to include. I'm proud of this."

The contributor must feel like the AUTHOR, not the data source. The AI structured their words, but the story, the observations, the quotes — those are theirs. Their name is on it. Their community reads it.

### The Reader

**Opening the article:** "This is about my town. This actually happened here."

**Reading it:** "This is well-written. It has quotes from real people I know. It tells me something I didn't know. It's interesting."

**Finishing it:** "I wish someone had written about [other thing that happened]. Maybe I should."

The reader should feel the same way they feel reading a good local newspaper — informed, connected to their community, and aware that someone cares enough to write things down.

### The Community

**Seeing the newspaper:** "Our town has a newspaper again. Someone is paying attention to what happens here."

**Reading multiple articles:** "Different people wrote these. Different perspectives. This isn't one person's blog — this is our community telling its own story."

**Over time:** "The articles are getting better. People are covering things that used to go unnoticed. The school board knows someone might write about their meeting. The new business got a welcome article. We're visible again."

The community should feel PROUD of their newspaper. Not "we have an AI that writes articles" but "we have neighbors who care enough to document our town, and the articles are genuinely good."

## What This Means for the Engine

Every design decision flows from these feelings:

**The AI asks, never demands.** Suggestions are phrased as questions and possibilities, never as requirements or errors. "Would you like to add..." not "You must include..."

**The AI celebrates what's good.** Before listing gaps, acknowledge what works. "The quote from Maria is excellent — it really captures the frustration." Then: "One more voice would make this complete."

**The bar is low to start, high to reach.** A 60-second voice memo produces a publishable article. But the AI shows you how a 90-second addition would make it twice as good. The contributor chooses how far to go.

**Quality is visible but not shaming.** The score is a guide, not a grade. 58/100 doesn't mean "you failed." It means "here's how to make it even better." And 58/100 is still publishable — it's better than no coverage at all.

**The contributor's name, always.** Every article says "By [Name]" prominently. The AI is invisible. The contributor gets full credit. When their neighbor says "I read your article," that moment is the product.

**Local pride is the metric.** Not engagement. Not clicks. Not time-on-site. Would the people in this town be proud to show this newspaper to someone from outside? That's the bar.

## The Difference

Most AI writing tools optimize for: speed, volume, SEO, engagement.

We optimize for: the contributor feels proud, the reader feels interested, the community feels represented.

That's not a tagline. It's the engineering spec. Every prompt, every UI decision, every feedback message is tested against: does this make the contributor prouder of their work? Does this make the reader more interested? Does this make the community more visible?
