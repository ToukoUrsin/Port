## MOVE #11: Council Minutes Demo (READY)

### Source Material Found

**Pelkosenniemi Municipal Board Meeting** — January 19, 2026
- Population: ~900 people. Smallest municipality in mainland Finland. No resident journalist.
- Meeting ran from 15:36 to 20:01 (4.5 hours!)
- 23 agenda items

**The juiciest items to demo on stage:**

1. **Tourist Tax** (Section 13) — The Finnish government is exploring a regional tourist tax for Northern/Eastern Finland. Pelkosenniemi (home of Pyha-Luosto ski resort) gave their official statement. This directly affects local tourism businesses and residents. Nobody outside the municipality knows about this.

2. **School Transport Dispute** (Section 5) — A taxi company (Taksi Petteri Kokko Oy) was excluded from the school transport bidding process and appealed to the Market Court. The company was dropped because their representative committed "a serious professional misconduct error" in a previous school transport contract, failed to provide required documentation, left the VAT register, and didn't even have a registered board of directors. The municipality had to make emergency taxi contracts to keep kids getting to school. Drama affecting every family with school-age children — unreported.

3. **Official Announcement Newspaper** (Section 14) — The municipality chose Koti-Lappi as their official announcement paper for 2026. Koti-Lappi became a paid subscription paper in 2025 (was previously free). This means fewer residents now see official municipal announcements. The irony: the municipality is PAYING to announce things to people who may not see them anymore.

4. **East Lapland Economic Vitality Program** (Section 15) — Regional economic development plan for 2025-2029.

5. **Emergency Preparedness Working Group** (Section 16) — Establishing a new emergency preparedness and readiness planning group.

### Demo Script for Stage

Show the PDF on screen — attendance roll, bureaucratic formatting, 30 pages of Finnish legalese.

> "This is the Pelkosenniemi municipal board meeting from January 19th. 900 residents. No journalist. This meeting ran for four and a half hours. They discussed a tourist tax that affects every business in the region, a school transport crisis that affects every family with children, and their official newspaper going behind a paywall. Nobody reported on any of it."
