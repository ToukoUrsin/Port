# PITCH SCRIPT — 3 Minutes

> TARGET: PORT_2026 judges. Criteria: Technical Feasibility (10), Market Potential (10), Team (5), Risk (5).
> CONSTRAINT: 3 minutes. Judges decide in 60 seconds. Lead with feeling, follow with proof.

---

## [0:00 - 0:15] THE HOOK — Problem First

"You read 10 articles about AI regulation this week. You covered 3 perspectives. You missed 6. And the one nobody — not you, not the NYT, not anyone — is talking about? The environmental cost of the AI systems being built to enforce the regulation itself.

You didn't know you were blind. That's the problem."

## [0:15 - 0:25] THE GAP

"Every article you read, every video you watch, every post you share — you have no idea how complete your picture is. Food has nutrition labels. Buildings have safety codes. Cars have emissions ratings. Media has nothing."

## [0:25 - 0:35] THE PRODUCT

"We built Blind Spot. Paste any article — or your own draft — and in 3 seconds you know: what perspectives are covered, what's missing, and the one blind spot that should keep you up at night."

## [0:35 - 1:00] THE DEMO

[Paste article URL. Click analyze. Report fills in.]

"33%. The New York Times. Three out of nine perspectives."

[Coverage bar grows to 33%. Covered perspectives check off. Missing ones appear one by one.]

"And look — click any missing one."

[Click 'labor displacement' — a paragraph appears: what this perspective argues, with sources.]

"You don't just see the gap. You hear the voice that was missing."

## [1:00 - 1:10] THE PERSONAL MOMENT

"I checked my own draft before this pitch. I was at 25%. I'd been working on it for two weeks. I had no idea."

## [1:10 - 1:30] THE BUSINESS

"Grammarly checks your grammar — 30 million users. We check your blind spots. Same model: score, specific items, one-click to fix. $15 a month for creators. $500 for newsroom teams. API for platforms."

## [1:30 - 2:10] THE MARKET

"Blind Spot is not a fact-checker. Fact-checkers are reactive — they check after something spreads. Blind Spot is structural — it shows you the landscape before you get lost in it.

We mapped 150 existing tools. Some show quality ratings. Some show structure. Nobody shows both. And nobody shows what's absent.

Here's why this is a business. When email hit 90% spam, the solution wasn't teaching people to read better — it was automated curation. Media is in the same crisis. Blind Spot is the curation layer.

Three markets. First: education. Schools need media literacy tools that SHOW, not lecture. Blind Spot IS the lesson — students see bias and gaps instead of being told about them.

Second: newsrooms. Editors use our API to audit their own coverage — what perspectives are we missing?

Third: platforms. Embed perspective diversity signals the way nutrition labels are embedded on food. And here's what we know about nutrition labels — they changed the food industry even though most consumers ignore them. Producers reformulated to look good on the label. Visible quality signals change how content is MADE, not just consumed."

## [2:10 - 2:30] LIVE DEMO

"We pre-loaded that for speed. Let me run one live."

[Types new topic. Waits. Report appears.]

"Works on anything. Want to pick a topic?"

## [2:30 - 2:45] VALIDATION [if done]

"We tested this on [N] people here at the hackathon. [X] said they'd use it before publishing. Most common reaction: 'I had no idea I was missing that.'"

## [2:45 - 3:00] THE CLOSE

"The real media crisis isn't misinformation. It's that we've lost the shared framework for figuring out what's true together. Not 'people disagree' — the process for resolving disagreement has broken down.

Every platform shows you content. We show you the shape of what's missing. This is Blind Spot."
