## Accuracy & AI

**"How accurate is the AI?"**
About 80%. That's sufficient. Spam filters started at 80% and made email usable. We're transparent about confidence levels. And 80% accurate structure is infinitely better than 0% structure, which is what exists today. Knowing 80% of your blind spots is better than knowing none. Every wrong suggestion teaches us — the model improves with use.

**"What if the AI is wrong?"**
The question isn't perfection — it's whether knowing most of your blind spots is better than knowing none. And every wrong suggestion teaches us — the model improves with use.

**"Isn't this just asking ChatGPT to analyze an article?"**
Try it. Ask ChatGPT "what perspectives am I missing on AI regulation." You'll get a generic list. We produce a SCORED report specific to your content, with the most surprising gap highlighted, and you can click to hear each missing voice. The prompt engineering and perspective taxonomy are the product.

**"Isn't this just another AI wrapper?"**
The AI is the engine, not the product. The product is the STRUCTURE — perspective decomposition, quality signals, gap detection. We did extensive research before writing a line of code. Nobody else does this because nobody else has the framework that generates it.

## Perspectives & Methodology

**"How do you know which perspectives exist?"**
We use a large language model to decompose any topic into its constituent perspectives — economic, legal, ethical, environmental, affected communities. It's not a fixed list. It's generated per topic.

**"Who decides what perspectives exist?" / "Who decides what's 'missing'?"**
The LLM proposes based on stakeholder analysis, disciplinary lenses, and scale. Users can add perspectives the system missed. It's exploratory, not authoritative — like a map, not a verdict. The AI identifies perspectives that exist in the broader discourse but are absent from the specific content you're checking. It's not prescriptive — it doesn't say you SHOULD cover everything. It says: here's what exists that you're not seeing.

## Competitive Landscape

**"How is this different from Ground News?"**
Ground News shows left versus right — one political axis. We show the full perspective space. Labor, environment, disability, indigenous, economic — perspectives that don't map to left-right. And we're for creators, not just readers.

**"Why not just use Community Notes / fact-checkers?"**
Community Notes is declining in output and locked to X. Fact-checkers are reactive (check after spread) and binary (true/false). We're structural (show the landscape) and continuous (scored, not verdicts). Different category entirely.

## Market & Business

**"Will people pay for this?"**
Grammarly charges $12/month to fix comma errors. We charge $15/month to fix blind spots. Creators already pay for tools that make their work better. And the real money is newsroom teams at $500/month — perspective auditing for editorial teams.

**"How do you make money?"**
API pricing for newsrooms and platforms. SaaS for schools (per-seat). Consumer freemium drives awareness and creates the demand for B2B. EdTech is the wedge — clearest pain, fastest sales cycle.

**"Will anyone actually change their behavior?"**
Nutrition labels changed the food industry even though most consumers never read them. Producers reformulated to look good on the label. When blind spots become visible, creators compete on completeness. The label changes the industry.

**"What's the market size?"**
Ground News has 250,000 paying users for a simpler version — left versus right. We go deeper. Not political axis. Full perspective space. The perspectives that don't fit on a left-right line.

## Risks & Adversarial Behavior

**"What about manipulation? Someone games the scores?"**
Same challenge as spam. Adversarial pressure is expected. The system improves through use — more perspectives, better calibration. The email spam industry is $5B because the adversarial game CREATES the market.

## Moat & Defensibility

**"What's the moat?"**
Two things. First: calibrated perspective taxonomies per topic domain, built from every analysis we run. After a year, nobody can replicate our map of which perspectives matter for which topics. Second: the creator workflow — once you check every draft, you don't stop. Retention is habitual.
