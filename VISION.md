# Vision

Local news is dead. 213 US counties have zero journalists. 50 million Americans live in communities where nobody writes down what happens. When a town loses its newspaper, corruption rises, voter turnout drops, public health violations go unreported, and community identity erodes. This is happening everywhere — the US, Finland, globally.

But the information isn't gone. People were at the town hall meeting. They saw the new business open. They know what happened at the school board. The knowledge exists — it's just locked in people's heads and scattered across Facebook posts that disappear tomorrow.

We give them a phone, AI, and a publish button.

---

## How It Works

Record a voice memo. Take some photos. Jot down what happened. The AI turns it into a real news article — structured, with attributed quotes, proper context. Then a second AI pass reviews it: are the facts grounded in what you actually said? Are any claims extreme? Whose perspective is missing from this story? The contributor sees what could be stronger, fixes what they want, and hits publish. Five minutes from event to published story on your town's digital newspaper.

Not citizen journalism with all its problems. AI-assisted, AI-reviewed citizen journalism — where the quality layer is built in, not bolted on.

---

## Why the Economics Are New

Local news failed because the cost structure was industrial. A small-town paper needed $200-500K a year: reporters, editors, office, printing, distribution. A town of 2,000 people can't support that.

Our cost to serve one town is under $5 a year. Contributors are community members, not employees. AI writing and review costs pennies per article. Hosting is negligible. Even $10 in local ad revenue makes a town profitable.

We don't need local news to be a big business per town. We need it to be a near-free service that scales across thousands of towns.

| | Traditional Paper | This Platform |
|---|---|---|
| Cost per town | $200-500K/year | ~$50-200/year |
| Revenue needed to survive | $200K+ | Almost nothing |

Revenue comes from local business ads (even small amounts work at near-zero cost), municipal subscriptions (towns pay for enhanced community communication), and eventually aggregate advertising across thousands of towns.

## The Quality Layer

The obvious question: what stops people from publishing garbage?

Every article is reviewed before publishing. The AI checks quotes against the transcript, flags claims that seem extreme, catches missing context. But it goes further — it asks whose voice is missing from the story. "You mentioned three teams but only quoted one. Consider adding another perspective." "This story speaks about the immigrant community without any voices from that community."

Coaching, not policing. The AI doesn't block you — it makes your story better. Like having the world's best editor in your pocket.

The old newspaper had one editor with one worldview. Our AI editor checks every story not just for accuracy, but for completeness. The town doesn't get its old newspaper back. It gets a better one.

---

## Why Now

1. AI writing crossed the threshold — LLMs can generate publication-quality articles from rough notes. This wasn't possible two years ago.
2. Transcription is near-free — voice-to-text at broadcast quality for $0.006/minute.
3. The crisis is accelerating — more papers close every month.
4. Community platforms are failing at news — Facebook deprioritized it. Nextdoor is social, not journalistic. The gap is wider than ever.
5. People already share local information for free — on Facebook groups, WhatsApp, Reddit. We just give it structure and reach.

---

## Where It Goes

**Phase 1:** English-speaking news deserts. US rural towns, UK, Australia. Prove the model works.

**Phase 2:** Any language. The pipeline works in 99 languages through Whisper and Claude. Finland's shrinking local papers. India's unserved villages. South America's rural communities.

**Phase 3:** Beyond geography. University campus newspapers. Company internal news. Religious communities. Any group where things happen but nobody writes them up.

**Phase 4:** The data layer. Thousands of towns producing stories daily becomes the most granular dataset of community-level activity worldwide. "Water quality complaints spiking in 47 Ohio towns" — state-level patterns surfaced from local data.

---

## Existing Validation

**Lokal (YC S19)** built a local news platform for India's small towns. Raised funding, proved local news works as a platform business in underserved markets. They succeeded by making content creation cheap through user-generated content with lightweight editorial. We go further — AI handles the editorial layer entirely.

---

## Risks We're Honest About

**Misinformation.** Someone will publish false claims. AI review catches obvious issues, but can't catch everything. The honest answer: the alternative is misinformation spreading on Facebook groups with zero review. We're strictly better than the status quo.

**Cold start.** An empty newspaper attracts nobody. We seed each town with AI-generated summaries from public records — council minutes, school calendars, police blotter. The newspaper works on day one. Contributors add to it.

**Contributor motivation.** People already share local information for free on social media. We're channeling energy that exists, not creating it. The difference: their contribution becomes a real article, not a post that disappears.

**AI hallucination.** The AI generates from the contributor's input — transcript, photos, notes. It structures, it doesn't invent. The review layer checks the article against the source material. Contributors review before publishing.

---

*Local news didn't die because people stopped caring about their communities. It died because the economics broke. AI fixes the economics. The people were always there. We just connect the pieces.*
